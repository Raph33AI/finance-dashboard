⭐ AlphaVault Score Engine

🧠 What is AlphaVault Score?

A proprietary normalized indicator (0-100) combining advanced machine learning analysis with technical indicators, momentum, and risk metrics.

📊 How It Works

Our engine trains 5 ML models on historical patterns (no raw data redistributed) and transforms predictions into:

  • Directional Signals: ↑ Bullish / ↓ Bearish / ⚖ Neutral
  • Percentage Changes: Expected movement magnitude
  • Risk-Adjusted Scores: Normalized 0-100 scale
  • Confidence Levels: High / Medium / Low

🎯 Score Components

  • 📈 Momentum (25%): Recent trend strength
  • 🧠 ML Consensus (30%): Model agreement
  • 📊 Technical (20%): RSI, MACD, Bollinger
  • 🎯 Volatility (15%): Risk-adjusted performance
  • ✨ Trend Quality (10%): R² accuracy

⚖ Legal Compliance

100% Compliant: No raw market data is redistributed. All outputs are proprietary analytical transformations.

⚠ Important: For educational purposes only. Not financial advice.

🎯 Ensemble Prediction

What is Ensemble Learning?

Ensemble methods combine multiple ML models (weighted by R² accuracy) to produce more reliable predictions than any single model.

AlphaVault Ensemble Score

Instead of showing raw price predictions, we calculate a normalized score (0-100) representing market sentiment and direction strength.

Score Interpretation:
• 75-100: Strong Bullish 🚀
• 60-74: Bullish 📈
• 40-59: Neutral ⚖
• 25-39: Bearish 📉
• 0-24: Strong Bearish ⚠
💡 Pro Tip: Higher confidence scores indicate stronger model consensus.

📈 Backtesting & Validation

What is Backtesting?

Backtesting validates model performance by testing predictions against historical outcomes (direction accuracy, not price accuracy to maintain compliance).

Key Metrics (Legal Compliant)

Measured Metrics:
• Direction Accuracy: % of correct ↑/↓ predictions
• MAPE: Mean Absolute Percentage Error
• R² Score: Model fit quality (0-100%)
💡 Pro Tip: Models with >70% direction accuracy are generally more reliable.

🔗 Model Correlation

Model Correlation Heatmap

Shows how similarly different models predict directional changes. High correlation = strong consensus.

Consensus Score

A single metric (0-100%) indicating overall model agreement.

Interpretation:
• >80%: High confidence (strong consensus)
• 60-80%: Moderate confidence
• <60%: Low confidence (mixed signals)
💡 Pro Tip: Low consensus often precedes market volatility.
