M&A Dashboard Overview

What is the M&A Dashboard?

The M&A Dashboard provides a real-time overview of merger and acquisition activity based on SEC EDGAR filings.

Key Metrics:

  • Critical Alerts: High-priority 8-K filings indicating potential M&A activity (Item 1.01, 2.01, 5.01)
  • Recent S-4 Filings: Merger/acquisition registration statements
  • DEFM14A Filings: Definitive merger proxy statements
  • Active Deals: Total number of ongoing M&A transactions
  • Average Deal Size: Mean transaction value (when available)
  • Top Sectors: Industries with highest M&A activity

Data Source:

All data is sourced directly from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) EDGAR database via official RSS feeds and filings.

8-K Material Agreement Alerts

What are 8-K Material Agreement Alerts?

Form 8-K is filed when a company experiences a "current event" that shareholders should know about. Certain items in 8-K filings are strong indicators of M&A activity.

Critical M&A Indicators (Item Numbers):

CRITICAL (Red Alerts)

  • Item 1.01 - Entry into Material Definitive Agreement:
    • Execution of merger/acquisition agreements
    • Typically filed within 4 business days of signing
    • Contains deal structure, price, and terms
  • Item 2.01 - Completion of Acquisition or Disposition:
    • Deal has closed
    • Assets or businesses have been transferred
    • Transaction is finalized
  • Item 5.01 - Changes in Control:
    • Ownership change (new shareholders with >5% stake)
    • Direct indicator of acquisition/takeover

HIGH PRIORITY (Orange Alerts)

  • Item 1.02 - Termination of Material Agreement: Deal fell through (important for arbitrage)
  • Item 5.02 - Director/Officer Changes: New management post-merger
  • Item 8.01 - Other Material Events: Voluntary disclosure of M&A discussions

How to Use:

  1. Filter by Priority: Focus on "Critical" for confirmed deals
  2. Check Filing Date: Recent filings (last 7-30 days) are most actionable
  3. Read Summary: Each alert shows detected M&A keywords and Item numbers
  4. Click for Details: View full SEC filing for complete information

Legal Note: Always read the full 8-K filing before making investment decisions. Alerts are automated and may require human verification.

M&A Probability Score

What is the M&A Probability Score?

A quantitative score (0-100%) that estimates the likelihood of a company being acquired or merging within the next 12 months.

Scoring Methodology:

Signal Weights:

Signal Weight Description
Unusual 8-K Activity 30% Material agreements, board meetings, legal counsel changes
Insider Selling Freeze 25% Sudden drop in Form 4 filings (blackout period)
Institutional Accumulation 20% Increased 13F holdings by large funds
Options Activity 15% Unusual call buying (requires external data)
Board Changes 5% New directors with M&A experience
Legal Counsel Changes 5% Hiring M&A specialist law firms

Score Interpretation:

  • 0-25%: Low Probability - Normal business operations
  • 25-50%: Moderate Probability - Some signals detected, monitor
  • 50-75%: High Probability - Multiple strong signals, elevated risk
  • 75-100%: Very High Probability - Deal likely imminent or in progress

Disclaimer: The M&A Probability Score is a predictive model based on historical patterns. It does not guarantee future M&A activity. Always perform your own due diligence.

Deal Comps Database

What are Deal Comps?

Deal Comparables (Comps) are historical M&A transactions used to benchmark valuation multiples for current deals.

Key Multiples:

  • EV/Sales: Enterprise Value / Revenue - Common for growth companies
  • EV/EBITDA: Enterprise Value / Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortization - Standard multiple
  • Takeover Premium: % above pre-announcement stock price

How to Use:

  1. Filter by sector to find relevant comparables
  2. Check recent deals (last 1-2 years) for current market conditions
  3. Compare multiples to target company's current valuation
  4. Use average premium to estimate potential takeover price

Data Source:

S-4 and DEFM14A filings from SEC EDGAR. Some multiples may require manual extraction from documents.

Serial Acquirers

What are Serial Acquirers?

Companies that consistently pursue growth through acquisitions. They typically have M&A as a core part of their corporate strategy.

Why Track Them?

  • Predictive Value: Knowing who the buyers are helps identify potential targets
  • Valuation Benchmarks: Serial acquirers establish price floors in sectors
  • Deal Flow: Active acquirers often have multiple deals in pipeline

Profile Metrics:

  • Acquisitions Count: Total deals in last 5 years
  • Preferred Sectors: Industries they target
  • Average Deal Size: Typical transaction value
  • Last Acquisition: Most recent deal date

Examples:

Technology: Microsoft, Oracle, Salesforce
Healthcare: Johnson & Johnson, UnitedHealth, CVS
Financial: JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America

Takeover Premium Calculator

What is a Takeover Premium?

The percentage above the current stock price that an acquirer typically pays to gain control of a target company.

How It's Calculated:

  1. Enter the target company's ticker and current stock price
  2. Select the industry sector
  3. Calculator uses historical sector-specific average premiums
  4. Provides Low/Mid/High estimates based on:
    • Low (70%): Friendly deals, limited competition
    • Mid (100%): Sector average
    • High (130%): Competitive bidding, strategic value

Typical Premiums by Sector:

  • Technology: 25-40% (high strategic value)
  • Healthcare: 30-50% (regulatory complexity)
  • Financial Services: 20-35% (regulatory hurdles)
  • Consumer: 25-35% (brand value)
  • Industrial: 20-30% (asset-based)

Important: These are estimates based on historical averages. Actual premiums vary widely based on company-specific factors, market conditions, and deal structure.

Regulatory Timeline Predictor

What is the Regulatory Timeline?

The estimated time required for government agencies (FTC, DOJ, EU Commission) to review and approve a merger or acquisition.

Review Phases:

1. Initial Filing (Month 1)

  • Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Act filing in the U.S.
  • EU merger notification (if applicable)
  • 30-day waiting period begins

2. FTC/DOJ Review (Months 1-3)

  • Antitrust analysis
  • Market concentration assessment
  • Competitive impact evaluation

3. Second Request (Months 3-6, if triggered)

  • Extensive document production
  • Depositions and interviews
  • Economic analysis
  • Only in ~3% of deals

4. Final Approval (Month 6-12)

  • Clearance or remedies (asset divestitures)
  • Consent decree (if conditions imposed)
  • Deal closes

Factors Affecting Timeline:

  • Deal Size: >$10B = Longer review (6-12 months)
  • Sector: Tech, Healthcare, Defense = High scrutiny
  • Market Share: Combined >40% = Likely challenge
  • Cross-Border: Multiple jurisdictions = Sequential reviews
  • Political Climate: Current administration's antitrust stance

Recent Trends (2023-2024):

  • FTC/DOJ more aggressive under current administration
  • Average review time increased 40% vs. 2015-2020
  • Tech deals face heightened scrutiny
  • ESG considerations emerging as factor

Note: Timelines are estimates. Some deals clear in 30 days, others take 18+ months or are blocked entirely. Always consult with legal counsel for specific transactions.
