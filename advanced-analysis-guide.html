1. Technical Indicators

Technical indicators are mathematical calculations based on price, volume, or open interest that help identify trends and potential reversal points.

RSI - Relative Strength Index

1

What is RSI?

RSI measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions. It oscillates between 0 and 100.

RSI Formula

RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))
where RS = Average Gain / Average Loss (over 14 periods)

2

Interpretation

RSI Value Signal Action
Above 70 Overbought Potential sell signal
50-70 Bullish momentum Hold or monitor
30-50 Bearish momentum Monitor closely
Below 30 Oversold Potential buy signal

Important: RSI can remain in overbought/oversold territory for extended periods during strong trends. Always combine with other indicators.

MACD - Moving Average Convergence Divergence

1

What is MACD?

MACD shows the relationship between two moving averages of a stock's price. It consists of three components:

  • MACD Line: 12-day EMA minus 26-day EMA
  • Signal Line: 9-day EMA of MACD Line
  • Histogram: MACD Line minus Signal Line
2

Trading Signals

  • Bullish Crossover: MACD line crosses above signal line (buy signal)
  • Bearish Crossover: MACD line crosses below signal line (sell signal)
  • Divergence: Price and MACD move in opposite directions (reversal warning)

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (20-day SMA) and two outer bands at 2 standard deviations above and below.

Squeeze

Bands narrow - low volatility. Breakout imminent.

Expansion

Bands widen - high volatility. Trend confirmed.

Upper Band Touch

Price at upper band - overbought condition.

Lower Band Touch

Price at lower band - oversold condition.

    3. Fundamental Analysis

    Key Valuation Metrics

    P/E

    Price-to-Earnings Ratio

    P/E = Stock Price / Earnings Per Share

    • Below 15: Potentially undervalued or low growth
    • 15-25: Fair value for mature companies
    • Above 25: Growth premium or overvalued

    Compare P/E to sector average. Tech stocks typically have higher P/E ratios than utilities.

    EPS

    Earnings Per Share

    EPS = (Net Income - Dividends) / Shares Outstanding

    • Growing EPS: Company increasing profitability
    • Declining EPS: Warning sign - investigate cause
    • EPS Beat: Actual > Estimate (bullish)
    • EPS Miss: Actual < Estimate (bearish)
    ROE

    Return on Equity

    ROE = Net Income / Shareholder Equity

    ROE Range Quality
    Above 20% Excellent - highly efficient
    15-20% Good - solid performer
    10-15% Average - acceptable
    Below 10% Weak - investigate further

    5. Risk Metrics

    Volatility (Standard Deviation)

    Measures price fluctuation. Higher volatility = higher risk.

    • Low volatility (< 20%): Stable stocks (utilities, consumer staples)
    • Medium volatility (20-40%): Most blue-chip stocks
    • High volatility (> 40%): Growth/tech stocks, small caps

    Beta

    Measures stock sensitivity to market movements.

    Beta Value Interpretation Example
    Beta = 1.0 Moves with market S&P 500 index fund
    Beta > 1.0 More volatile than market Tech stocks (NVDA: β=1.7)
    Beta < 1.0 Less volatile than market Utilities (DUK: β=0.4)
    Beta < 0 Inverse correlation Gold, VIX

    Sharpe Ratio

    Risk-adjusted return metric. Higher is better.

    Sharpe Ratio Formula

    Sharpe = (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Portfolio Standard Deviation

    Sharpe > 1.0 = Good
    Sharpe > 2.0 = Very Good
    Sharpe > 3.0 = Excellent
