What is Advanced Technical Analysis?

This page provides professional-grade technical indicators used by institutional traders to analyze price movements, trends, and market momentum.

üîç Available Indicators

  • Ichimoku Cloud: Multi-dimensional support/resistance with trend direction
  • Stochastic Oscillator: Momentum indicator comparing closing price to price range
  • Williams %R: Overbought/oversold momentum oscillator
  • ADX: Measures trend strength regardless of direction
  • Parabolic SAR: Stop and Reverse points for trend following
  • OBV: Volume-based momentum indicator
  • ATR: Volatility measurement for risk management
  • Fibonacci Retracements: Key support/resistance levels
  • Pivot Points: Intraday support/resistance calculations
  • VWAP: Volume-weighted average price for institutional levels

üìà How to Use

  1. Search for a stock by symbol or name
  2. Select analysis period (1M to 5Y)
  3. Click "Analyze Stock"
  4. Review consolidated signals and individual indicators
  5. Make informed trading decisions based on multiple confirmations
‚ö† Important: Technical analysis should be used in conjunction with fundamental analysis and risk management. No indicator is 100% accurate.

How Signals Are Calculated

The Consolidated Signal aggregates multiple technical indicators to provide an overall market sentiment.

Signal Categories

  • STRONG BUY (80-100%): Majority of indicators show bullish signals
  • BUY (60-80%): More bullish than bearish indicators
  • NEUTRAL (40-60%): Mixed signals, no clear direction
  • SELL (20-40%): More bearish than bullish indicators
  • STRONG SELL (0-20%): Majority of indicators show bearish signals

Indicators Analyzed

Each indicator contributes +1 (bullish), 0 (neutral), or -1 (bearish) to the final score:

  • Stochastic Oscillator (overbought/oversold levels)
  • Williams %R (momentum direction)
  • ADX (trend strength and direction)
  • Parabolic SAR (price vs SAR position)
  • OBV (volume trend)
  • VWAP (price vs institutional levels)
  • Ichimoku Cloud (price vs cloud position)
üí° Pro Tip: Wait for multiple confirmations across different indicator types before making trading decisions.

What is Ichimoku Cloud?

The Ichimoku Kinko Hyo (equilibrium chart at a glance) is a comprehensive indicator that defines support/resistance, identifies trend direction, gauges momentum, and provides trading signals.

Components

  • Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line): (9-period high + 9-period low) / 2
  • Kijun-sen (Base Line): (26-period high + 26-period low) / 2
  • Senkou Span A (Leading Span A): (Tenkan-sen + Kijun-sen) / 2, plotted 26 periods ahead
  • Senkou Span B (Leading Span B): (52-period high + 52-period low) / 2, plotted 26 periods ahead
  • Chikou Span (Lagging Span): Current closing price plotted 26 periods back
  • Kumo (Cloud): Area between Senkou Span A and B

How to Interpret

  • Price above Cloud: Bullish trend (buy zone)
  • Price below Cloud: Bearish trend (sell zone)
  • Price in Cloud: Consolidation/ranging market
  • Tenkan crosses above Kijun: Bullish signal (golden cross)
  • Tenkan crosses below Kijun: Bearish signal (death cross)
  • Green Cloud (Span A > Span B): Bullish support
  • Red Cloud (Span A < Span B): Bearish resistance
üí° Pro Tip: Ichimoku works best on daily charts and in trending markets. Avoid using it in choppy, sideways markets.

What is the Stochastic Oscillator?

The Stochastic Oscillator is a momentum indicator that compares a particular closing price to a range of prices over a certain period.

Calculation

%K = [(Current Close - Lowest Low) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)] √ó 100
%D = 3-period SMA of %K
Default period: 14

How to Interpret

  • Above 80: Overbought zone - potential sell signal
  • Below 20: Oversold zone - potential buy signal
  • %K crosses above %D: Bullish signal
  • %K crosses below %D: Bearish signal
‚ö† Warning: In strong trends, Stochastic can remain overbought/oversold for extended periods.

üìâ Williams %R

What is Williams %R?

The Williams %R is a momentum indicator that measures overbought/oversold levels, ranging from 0 to -100.

Calculation

Williams %R = [(Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)] √ó -100
Default period: 14

How to Interpret

  • -20 to 0: Overbought zone - consider selling
  • -80 to -100: Oversold zone - consider buying
  • -50: Centerline (neutral zone)

What is ADX?

The Average Directional Index (ADX) measures the strength of a trend, regardless of direction.

Components

  • ADX Line: Trend strength (0-100)
  • +DI: Upward movement strength
  • -DI: Downward movement strength

How to Interpret

  • ADX < 20: Weak trend, ranging market
  • ADX > 25: Strong trend
  • ADX > 40: Very strong trend
  • +DI above -DI + ADX > 25: Strong uptrend
  • -DI above +DI + ADX > 25: Strong downtrend

üéØ Parabolic SAR

What is Parabolic SAR?

The Parabolic SAR (Stop And Reverse) is a trend-following indicator that provides entry/exit points.

How to Interpret

  • Dots below price: Uptrend - stay long
  • Dots above price: Downtrend - stay short
  • Dots flip: Potential trend reversal
‚ö† Warning: Parabolic SAR generates many false signals in sideways markets.

üìä On-Balance Volume (OBV)

What is OBV?

The On-Balance Volume uses volume flow to predict changes in stock price.

How to Interpret

  • OBV rising + Price rising: Healthy uptrend
  • OBV falling + Price falling: Healthy downtrend
  • OBV rising + Price falling: Bullish divergence
  • OBV falling + Price rising: Bearish divergence

What is ATR?

The Average True Range measures market volatility.

How to Interpret

  • High ATR: High volatility - larger price swings
  • Low ATR: Low volatility - smaller movements
  • Rising ATR: Increasing volatility

Practical Applications

  • Set stops at 2-3√ó ATR from entry
  • Adjust position sizing based on ATR
  • Target 2-3√ó ATR for profit goals

What are Fibonacci Retracements?

Fibonacci Retracements are horizontal lines that indicate potential support/resistance levels.

Key Levels

  • 23.6%: Shallow retracement
  • 38.2%: Moderate retracement
  • 50%: Psychological midpoint
  • 61.8%: The "Golden Ratio" - most important
  • 78.6%: Deep retracement
üí° Pro Tip: Combine Fibonacci with other indicators for confirmation.

What are Pivot Points?

Pivot Points are technical indicators used to determine potential support and resistance levels.

Standard Pivot Points

Pivot (P) = (High + Low + Close) / 3
R1 = (2 √ó P) - Low
S1 = (2 √ó P) - High

How to Interpret

  • P (Pivot): Fair value
  • R1, R2, R3: Resistance levels
  • S1, S2, S3: Support levels

What is VWAP?

The Volume Weighted Average Price represents the "fair value" price where most institutional trading occurred.

How to Interpret

  • Price above VWAP: Bullish - institutional support
  • Price below VWAP: Bearish - institutional resistance
  • Price at VWAP: Fair value

Standard Deviation Bands

  • +1 SD: Normal upper range
  • +2 SD: Overbought
  • -1 SD: Normal lower range
  • -2 SD: Oversold
