1. Portfolio Construction

Core-Satellite Strategy

The core-satellite approach combines passive and active investing for optimal risk-adjusted returns.

Core Holdings (60-80%)

Low-cost index funds, ETFs. Provides market exposure and stability.

Satellite Holdings (20-40%)

Individual stocks, sector plays. Aims to outperform the market.

Asset Allocation by Risk Profile

Profile Stocks Bonds Cash Alternative
Conservative 30% 50% 15% 5%
Moderate 60% 30% 5% 5%
Aggressive 85% 5% 0% 10%
Very Aggressive 95% 0% 0% 5%

Rule of Thumb: Stock allocation = 110 - Your Age. A 30-year-old would allocate 80% to stocks (110-30=80).

2. Diversification Strategies

The 5 Dimensions of Diversification

1

Asset Class Diversification

Spread across stocks, bonds, real estate, commodities, and cash.

  • Stocks: Growth and dividends
  • Bonds: Income and stability
  • Real Estate: Inflation hedge
  • Commodities: Portfolio insurance
2

Sector Diversification

Don't overweight any single sector. Recommended maximum: 25% per sector.

Sector Cyclicality Beta
Technology High 1.3-1.8
Healthcare Low 0.8-1.1
Financials High 1.1-1.4
Consumer Staples Low 0.6-0.9
Energy Very High 1.4-2.0
3

Geographic Diversification

Reduce country-specific risk.

  • US Markets: 50-60%
  • Developed International: 25-35%
  • Emerging Markets: 10-15%
4

Market Cap Diversification

  • Large Cap (>10B): 60% - stability
  • Mid Cap (2-10B): 25% - growth potential
  • Small Cap (<2B): 15% - high growth
5

Temporal Diversification

Use Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) to reduce timing risk.

DCA Example

Instead of investing $12,000 at once, invest $1,000/month for 12 months.

Benefit: Averages out market volatility
Reduces risk of buying at peak

Over-Diversification Warning: More than 30 individual stocks provides diminishing returns. Focus on quality over quantity.

3. Portfolio Rebalancing

When to Rebalance

Time-Based

Rebalance quarterly or semi-annually regardless of drift.

Threshold-Based

Rebalance when allocation drifts >5% from target.

Hybrid Approach

Check quarterly, rebalance if drift >5%.

AI-Triggered

AlphaVault alerts when optimal rebalancing conditions are met.

Rebalancing Example

Asset Target Current Drift Action
US Stocks 60% 68% +8% Sell $8,000
International 25% 20% -5% Buy $5,000
Bonds 15% 12% -3% Buy $3,000

Historical Data: Annual rebalancing improved returns by 0.4-0.8% annually while reducing volatility by 12% (1990-2020).

4. Portfolio Optimization

Modern Portfolio Theory (Markowitz)

Our Markowitz optimizer finds the portfolio with the highest Sharpe ratio for your selected assets.

Optimization Objective
  • Inputs:
    • Historical returns (3-5 years)
    • Covariance matrix (asset correlations)
    • Expected returns (AI-predicted)
    • Risk-free rate (current T-Bill rate)
  • Optimization Process:
    • Calculate efficient frontier
    • Identify maximum Sharpe ratio point
    • Apply constraints (min/max weights)
  • Output:
    • Optimal weight for each asset
    • Expected portfolio return
    • Expected portfolio volatility
    • Sharpe ratio

    • Risk Parity Approach

    Alternative to Markowitz: equalize risk contribution instead of capital allocation.

    When to Use: Risk Parity works better in low-return environments and provides better downside protection than traditional 60/40 portfolios.
