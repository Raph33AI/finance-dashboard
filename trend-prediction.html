🎯 Ensemble Prediction

What is Ensemble Learning?

Ensemble methods combine multiple machine learning models to produce better predictions than any individual model.

How We Calculate It

The ensemble prediction is a weighted average of all models, where weights are determined by each model's historical accuracy (R² score).

Formula:
Ensemble = Σ (Predictioni × Weighti) / Σ Weighti
Where Weighti = R²i (model accuracy)

Confidence Range

The 95% confidence range shows where we expect the actual price to fall with 95% probability, calculated from the standard deviation of all model predictions.

💡 Pro Tip: Higher accuracy and tighter confidence ranges indicate more reliable predictions.

📈 Backtesting & Accuracy Analysis

What is Backtesting?

Backtesting evaluates how well our models would have performed in the past by comparing historical predictions with actual price movements.

How It Works

We train models on historical data, then test their predictions against real prices that occurred afterward. This reveals true model accuracy.

Key Metrics:
• MAPE: Mean Absolute Percentage Error
• Direction Accuracy: % of correct up/down predictions
• R² Score: Model fit quality (0-100%)

Why It Matters

Backtesting helps you understand which models are most reliable for your investment decisions and identify consistent performers.

💡 Pro Tip: Models with >70% direction accuracy and low MAPE are generally more trustworthy.

🎯 Multi-Horizon Predictions

What are Multi-Horizon Predictions?

Instead of showing a single timeframe, we display simultaneous predictions for 7, 15, 30, and 60 days ahead.

Fan Chart Visualization

The "fan" shape represents increasing uncertainty over time—short-term predictions are more confident than long-term ones.

Interpretation:
• Tight fan: High confidence across all horizons
• Wide fan: Greater uncertainty in longer timeframes
• Converging lines: Strong consensus between horizons
💡 Pro Tip: Use multi-horizon to plan entry/exit points at different investment durations.

🔗 Model Correlation & Consensus

What is Model Correlation?

The heatmap shows how similar the predictions from different models are. High correlation means strong agreement.

Consensus Score

A single metric (0-100%) indicating overall agreement between all models. Higher scores = more reliable predictions.

Color Guide:
• Dark Blue: High correlation (>0.8)
• Light Blue: Moderate correlation (0.5-0.8)
• White/Yellow: Low correlation (<0.5)

How to Use

When consensus is high (>80%), you can trust the ensemble prediction more confidently. Low consensus suggests mixed signals—proceed with caution.

💡 Pro Tip: Divergent models often signal market uncertainty or upcoming volatility.

📊 Multi-Stock Comparison

What is Multi-Stock Comparison?

This feature allows you to compare predictions and risk metrics for multiple stocks simultaneously using advanced ML ensemble models.

Key Features

  • Prediction Comparison: Side-by-side predictions across different horizons
  • Risk-Return Scatter: Visualize risk vs. expected return for each stock
  • Correlation Matrix: Identify diversification opportunities
  • Sharpe Ratio: Compare risk-adjusted returns

How to Use

  1. Add 2-10 stocks using the input field
  2. Adjust analysis parameters (volatility, correlation, risk-free rate, horizon)
  3. Click "Run Multi-Stock Analysis"
  4. Review comparative charts and metrics
💡 Pro Tip: Use this to build a diversified portfolio by selecting stocks with low correlation and high Sharpe ratios.
