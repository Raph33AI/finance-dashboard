Core Methodology

4 Principles, 1 Balanced Portfolio

Our Risk Parity engine equalizes risk contributions across all assets for optimal diversification

Inverse Volatility

Allocate weights inversely proportional to asset volatility. Lower volatility assets receive higher weights, ensuring each asset contributes equally to portfolio risk. Formula: wi ∝ 1/σi.

Correlation Matrix

Build complete covariance matrix capturing pairwise asset correlations (-1 to +1). Accounts for diversification benefits beyond simple volatility weighting. σij = ρijσiσj.

Risk Contribution

Calculate marginal risk contribution for each asset: RCi = wi(Σw) / σp. Target equal risk contributions across all positions (e.g., 33.3% each for 3 assets).

Optimization Engine

Iteratively optimize weights to minimize risk contribution dispersion while maintaining full investment (Σwi = 1). Compares against equal-weight benchmark for volatility reduction measurement.

Portfolio Analytics

Professional Risk Analytics

6 advanced analytics modules to optimize, balance, and monitor portfolio risk

Covariance Matrix

Build complete n×n covariance matrix from asset volatilities and correlations. Diagonal elements: σi², off-diagonal: ρijσiσj. Foundation for risk parity optimization.

Portfolio Volatility

Calculate total portfolio standard deviation: σp = √(w'Σw). Compares Risk Parity vs Equal Weight to measure volatility reduction percentage.

Risk Contributions

Compute marginal and component risk contributions for each asset. Visualize risk balance with column charts showing target equal contributions (e.g., 25% for 4 assets).

Diversification Benefit

Measure portfolio concentration vs equal weight baseline. Calculate: (1 - max(wi) / equal_weight) × 100%. Higher values indicate better diversification.

Allocation Comparison

Side-by-side pie charts: Risk Parity vs Equal Weight allocations. Interactive donut charts with asset-level weights, color-coded by volatility contribution.

Balance Quality Score

Quantify risk balancing effectiveness: 100 - (avg_deviation / target × 100). Scores 90%+ indicate excellent risk parity, 70-90%: good, <70%: imbalanced.
