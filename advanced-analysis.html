üìê What is Ichimoku?

The Ichimoku Kinko Hyo (Equilibrium Chart) is a comprehensive indicator that shows support/resistance, momentum, and trend direction in one view.

üìä Components

  • Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line): (9-period high + low) / 2
  • Kijun-sen (Base Line): (26-period high + low) / 2
  • Senkou Span A (Leading Span A): (Tenkan + Kijun) / 2, plotted 26 periods ahead
  • Senkou Span B (Leading Span B): (52-period high + low) / 2, plotted 26 periods ahead
  • Chikou Span (Lagging Span): Close plotted 26 periods back
  • Kumo (Cloud): Area between Senkou Span A and B

üéØ Interpretation

  • Price above Cloud: Bullish trend üìà
  • Price below Cloud: Bearish trend üìâ
  • Price in Cloud: Neutral / Ranging
  • Tenkan crosses above Kijun: Buy signal üü¢
  • Tenkan crosses below Kijun: Sell signal üî¥
  • Green Cloud: Support zone
  • Red Cloud: Resistance zone
üí° Trading Strategy: Only trade in the direction of the cloud. Use Tenkan/Kijun crossovers for entry/exit.

üìê What is Stochastic?

The Stochastic Oscillator compares a closing price to its price range over a period. Identifies overbought/oversold conditions.

üìà Components

  • %K (Fast Line): Current close position in range
  • %D (Slow Line): 3-period MA of %K

üéØ Signals

  • > 80: Overbought ‚ö†Ô∏è
  • < 20: Oversold üöÄ
  • %K crosses above %D: Buy üü¢
  • %K crosses below %D: Sell üî¥
Formula:
%K = 100 √ó [(C - L14) / (H14 - L14)]
%D = SMA(%K, 3)

Williams %R is a momentum indicator that measures overbought/oversold levels. Similar to Stochastic but inverted.

üéØ Interpretation

üìä ADX - Average Directional Index

×

üìê What is ADX?

ADX measures trend strength (not direction). Values range from 0-100.

üéØ Interpretation

  • ADX > 25: Strong trend

  • ADX < 20: Weak/No trend
  • +DI > -DI: Uptrend
  • -DI > +DI: Downtrend
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: ADX tells you IF there's a trend, not WHICH direction!

üéØ How to Use

üìä On-Balance Volume (OBV)

×

üìê What is OBV?

OBV uses volume flow to predict price changes. Cumulative indicator.

üéØ Interpretation

  • OBV rising + Price rising: Strong uptrend ‚úÖ
  • OBV falling + Price falling: Strong downtrend ‚úÖ
  • OBV diverges from price: Potential reversal ‚ö†Ô∏è
Formula:
If Close > Close[yesterday]: OBV = OBV[yesterday] + Volume
If Close < Close[yesterday]: OBV = OBV[yesterday] - Volume
If Close = Close[yesterday]: OBV = OBV[yesterday]

üìê What is ATR?

ATR measures market volatility. Higher ATR = higher volatility.

üéØ How to Use

  • Set Stop-Loss: Use 2-3√ó ATR from entry

üìä Fibonacci Retracements

×

üìê What are Fibonacci Levels?

Fibonacci retracements identify potential support/resistance levels based on the Golden Ratio.

üìä Key Levels

  • 0%: Start of move (high/low)
  • 23.6%: Shallow retracement
  • 38.2%: Moderate retracement
  • 50%: Mid-point (psychological level)
  • 61.8%: Golden Ratio - strongest level
  • 78.6%: Deep retracement
  • 100%: Full retracement

üéØ How to Trade

  1. Identify significant swing high/low
  2. Draw Fibonacci from low to high (uptrend) or high to low (downtrend)
  3. Watch for price reactions at key levels
  4. Enter on bounces at 38.2%, 50%, or 61.8%
üí° Pro Tip: 61.8% is the most reliable level. If broken, trend may be reversing.

üìê What are Pivot Points?

Pivot Points are used by floor traders to identify potential support/resistance levels for the day.

üìä Types of Pivots

1Ô∏è‚É£ Standard Pivots

Most common. Based on previous period's high, low, close.

  • Pivot (P): (High + Low + Close) / 3
  • R1, R2, R3: Resistance levels
  • S1, S2, S3: Support levels

2Ô∏è‚É£ Fibonacci Pivots

3Ô∏è‚É£ Camarilla Pivots

Emphasizes levels close to current price. Popular for intraday trading.

üéØ How to Trade

  • Price above Pivot ‚Üí Bias bullish
  • Price below Pivot ‚Üí Bias bearish
  • Use R/S levels for entries/exits
  • Break of R3 or S3 = strong trend

üìä VWAP - Volume-Weighted Average Price

×

üìê What is VWAP?

VWAP is the average price weighted by volume. Used by institutions as a benchmark for execution quality.

üéØ How to Use

  • Price above VWAP: Bullish - buyers in control üìà
  • Price below VWAP: Bearish - sellers in control üìâ
  • Price at VWAP: Fair value / equilibrium
  • VWAP + Std Dev Bands: Dynamic support/resistance

üíº Institutional Use

Institutions try to execute trades near VWAP to avoid moving the market. Traders can use this knowledge:

  • Buy when price dips below VWAP (institutional support)
  • Sell when price rises above VWAP (institutional resistance)
üí° Best Timeframe: Intraday trading (resets daily). Most effective on high volume stocks.

üìä Volume & Volatility Analysis

×

üéØ Why Volume Matters

Volume confirms price action. High volume = strong conviction, low volume = weak moves.

üìà Volume Principles

  • Volume precedes price: Volume often leads price movements
  • Confirmation: Breakouts need high volume to be valid
  • Divergence: Price up + volume down = weakness

üìä Volatility Insights

  • High volatility = higher risk/reward
  • Low volatility often precedes breakouts
  • Use ATR for position sizing and stop-loss placement

üìê Support & Resistance Analysis

×

üéØ What is Support & Resistance?

Support: Price level where buying interest prevents further decline.
Resistance: Price level where selling interest prevents further rise.

üìä Types of S/R

  • Horizontal S/R: Previous highs/lows
  • Trendlines: Dynamic support/resistance
  • Moving Averages: Dynamic levels
  • Fibonacci Levels: Mathematical retracements
  • Pivot Points: Calculated daily levels
  • Psychological Levels: Round numbers (e.g., 100, 150, 200)

‚úÖ Trading Strategy

  • Buy at support, sell at resistance
  • Wait for confirmation (candlestick patterns, volume)
  • When support breaks, it becomes resistance (and vice versa)
