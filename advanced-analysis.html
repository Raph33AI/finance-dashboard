What is the Stochastic Oscillator?

The Stochastic Oscillator is a momentum indicator that compares a particular closing price to a range of prices over a certain period.

Calculation

%K = [(Current Close - Lowest Low) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)] Ã— 100
%D = 3-period SMA of %K
Default period: 14

How to Interpret

  • Above 80: Overbought zone - potential sell signal
  • Below 20: Oversold zone - potential buy signal
  • %K crosses above %D: Bullish signal
  • %K crosses below %D: Bearish signal

What is Williams %R?

The Williams %R is a momentum indicator that measures overbought/oversold levels, ranging from 0 to -100.

Calculation

Williams %R = [(Highest High - Close) / (Highest High - Lowest Low)] Ã— -100
Default period: 14

How to Interpret

  • -20 to 0: Overbought zone - consider selling
  • -80 to -100: Oversold zone - consider buying
  • -50: Centerline (neutral zone)

What is ADX?

The Average Directional Index (ADX) measures the strength of a trend, regardless of direction.

Components

  • ADX Line: Trend strength (0-100)
  • +DI: Upward movement strength
  • -DI: Downward movement strength

How to Interpret

  • ADX < 20: Weak trend, ranging market
  • ADX > 25: Strong trend
  • ADX > 40: Very strong trend
  • +DI above -DI + ADX > 25: Strong uptrend
  • -DI above +DI + ADX > 25: Strong downtrend

What is Parabolic SAR?

The Parabolic SAR (Stop And Reverse) is a trend-following indicator that provides entry/exit points.

How to Interpret

  • Dots below price: Uptrend - stay long
  • Dots above price: Downtrend - stay short
  • Dots flip: Potential trend reversal
âš  Warning: Parabolic SAR generates many false signals in sideways markets.

What is OBV?

The On-Balance Volume uses volume flow to predict changes in stock price.

How to Interpret

  • OBV rising + Price rising: Healthy uptrend
  • OBV falling + Price falling: Healthy downtrend
  • OBV rising + Price falling: Bullish divergence
  • OBV falling + Price rising: Bearish divergence

The Average True Range measures market volatility.

  • Low ATR: Low volatility - smaller movements
  • Rising ATR: Increasing volatility

What are Fibonacci Retracements?

Fibonacci Retracements are horizontal lines that indicate potential support/resistance levels.

Key Levels

  • 23.6%: Shallow retracement
  • 38.2%: Moderate retracement
  • 50%: Psychological midpoint
  • 61.8%: The "Golden Ratio" - most important
  • 78.6%: Deep retracement
